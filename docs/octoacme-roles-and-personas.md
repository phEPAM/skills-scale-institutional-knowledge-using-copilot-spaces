# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and execute quality strategies to ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They manage testing activities, coordinate with development teams, and own quality metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategy and test plans for each feature
- Manage QA team and coordinate testing activities
- Establish and maintain quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Conduct manual and automated testing before releases
- Report quality metrics and identify quality risks
- Work with developers to resolve defects and improve testability

### Goals
- Ensure all features meet acceptance criteria before release
- Reduce production defects through comprehensive testing
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Enable faster, more confident deployments

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria reviews
- Daily standups with development team
- Quality reports in weekly syncs
- Test case documentation and defect tracking

### How QA Lead Interacts with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Reviews acceptance criteria, provides test feedback, identifies edge cases
- **With Product Managers**: Validates feature requirements align with quality standards
- **With Project Managers**: Reports on quality metrics and testing blockers
- **With Technical Architect**: Assesses testability of technical designs

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects design system architecture, assess technical feasibility, and guide technical decisions. They ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with enterprise standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design system architecture and technical solutions
- Assess technical feasibility of features and requirements
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Guide developers on technical best practices and patterns
- Review technical designs for scalability and maintainability
- Ensure compliance with security and performance standards

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable technical solutions
- Reduce technical debt and complexity
- Minimize technical risks and rework
- Enable efficient team scaling and knowledge sharing

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Weekly syncs with developers and product managers
- Technical risk assessment in risk registers
- Architecture documentation and decision logs

### How Technical Architect Interacts with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Provides technical guidance, reviews designs, mentors on best practices
- **With Product Managers**: Discusses technical feasibility and trade-offs
- **With Project Managers**: Identifies technical risks and dependencies
- **With QA Lead**: Ensures designs support testability

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads ensure projects meet security and compliance requirements. They conduct security reviews, manage vulnerability tracking, and partner with all teams on security best practices and incident response.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements and review acceptance criteria
- Conduct security code reviews and design reviews
- Manage vulnerability scanning and remediation
- Ensure compliance with security policies and standards
- Lead security incident response and post-incident reviews
- Provide security training and guidance to team members

### Goals
- Deliver secure, compliant solutions
- Minimize security vulnerabilities and breaches
- Enable rapid, secure incident response
- Foster security-conscious culture across teams

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews during planning and development
- Weekly security updates in project syncs
- Vulnerability reports and remediation tracking
- Incident response communications

### How Security Lead Interacts with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Reviews code for security, provides remediation guidance
- **With Product Managers**: Defines security requirements for features
- **With Project Managers**: Escalates security risks and compliance issues
- **With Technical Architect**: Ensures security by design in technical solutions
- **With QA Lead**: Coordinates security testing and penetration testing

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters (or Agile Coaches) facilitate team agility, remove blockers, and ensure the team follows agreed processes and ceremonies. They enable the team to operate efficiently and continuously improve.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove process blockers and impediments
- Track sprint metrics and team velocity
- Ensure the team follows Definition of Done and quality standards
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Support cross-team collaboration and dependency management

### Goals
- Enable high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Foster continuous improvement culture
- Reduce process friction and unplanned work

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Impediment and blocker tracking
- Sprint metrics and retrospective reports
- Coaching and mentoring interactions

### How Scrum Master Interacts with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Removes blockers, facilitates ceremonies, tracks progress
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinates sprint schedules and escalations
- **With Product Managers**: Ensures backlog is well-defined and prioritized
- **With QA Lead**: Coordinates testing activities within sprint
- **With Technical Architect**: Communicates technical dependencies and risks
